Swim Ontario · Masters

Masters Survey 2026 — Brief Summary

Fielded: May 2026 · Responses: 284 · Follow-up consent: 194 (68%)

The headline

Masters swimmers are highly satisfied and emotionally invested in their sport, but they want more competition opportunities close to home, better pool access, and lower costs. The most common word respondents used to describe their experience was "fun," followed by "community."

Overall satisfaction is strong: 72% are satisfied or very satisfied (mean 4.1 / 5). Satisfaction is highest with their own club's communication and coaching and lowest with pool access — the only area where fewer than half (51%) are satisfied.

What members want most (ranked priorities)

When asked to rank five strategic priorities, respondents put more competitions & events first (99 of 273 ranked it #1), followed by club growth support and better coach resources.

  1. More competitions & events (mean rank 2.56)
  2. Club growth support (2.79)
  3. Better coach resources (3.02)
  4. Community connection & networking (3.27)
  5. Increased promotion / visibility (3.36)

Top barriers to participation

  1. Limited pool time — 162 (57% of respondents)
  2. Work / schedule conflicts — 97
  3. No barriers for me — 63
  4. High membership fees — 63
  5. Lack of awareness / promotion — 38

The single most-requested change

Of 272 written responses, competitions and meets dominated (99 mentions) — especially more regional meets within driving distance, easier meet registration, and beginner-friendly events. The next most common asks were club/program growth (49), coaching (33), lower costs (31), and more pool access (30).

"More events in eastern Ontario. I cannot travel for competitions and all of them seem to be in the Toronto area."

"Keep costs down to allow greater participation."

It's about where the meets are, not how many

Even with a record competition calendar this season (nearly one meet per month), the demand for meets isn't a call for more volume — it's a call for meets closer to home. When the swimmers who want "more events close to home" are broken out by region, the request tracks distance from the GTA almost perfectly:

Region % wanting local meets
North East 73%
Western 70%
Eastern 67%
Central (GTA) 46%

Outside Central, 64% want more events close to home versus 46% inside it — an 18-point gap. Meets are already concentrated where Central swimmers live; the clear next step is sanctioned meets in the Western, Eastern, and North East regions so swimmers can compete within a reasonable drive. (North East is based on 11 respondents — directional; Western and Eastern are firmer.)

Five recommendations

  1. Expand regional competition — add meets outside the GTA so swimmers in the East, North East, Western and Huronian regions can compete within a reasonable drive.
  2. Advocate for pool access — be the provincial voice for lane time and facility funding; this is the clear satisfaction gap.
  3. Address cost & affordability — review membership/entry fees and the MSO/Swim Ontario fee structure to remove a barrier for non-competitive swimmers.
  4. Support club growth & newcomer pathways — many clubs are full; help start new clubs and create a clear, welcoming on-ramp for beginners and triathlon crossovers.
  5. Improve Swim Ontario's communication & meet registration — simplify registration and raise awareness, the two weakest spots in the otherwise positive communication picture. Email newsletter is the preferred channel (94% of respondents).
