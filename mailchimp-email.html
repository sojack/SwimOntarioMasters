Masters Swimming Newsletter — July 2026
 

Hello Masters swimmers,

And just like that, the 2025–26 season is nearly a wrap. With Provincials, the Speedo Canadian Masters Championships, and the Worlds Warm-Up now behind us, just one event remains on the calendar — the Ontario Open Water Championships in Gravenhurst. It’s been a remarkable season: close to a meet a month across the province. Thank you for showing up, racing hard, and making the deck such a good place to be.

When we asked what Masters swimming means to you in this year’s survey, the word you used most often was fun — closely followed by community. You’ll find both threaded through this issue: awards to celebrate the people who make our sport better, a survey that’s still listening, a new way to get involved, and a first look at where the road leads in 2026 and 2027.

Let’s dive in. — The Swim Ontario Masters committee

Your voice · Survey still open

Have your say: the 2026 Masters Survey

Nearly 300 of you have already shared how Masters swimming is going — and the picture is a good one. A few things we’ve heard so far:

  • 72% are satisfied or very satisfied with their Masters experience (4.1 / 5 overall).
  • The words that came up most? “Fun” and “community.”
  • Your top request isn’t more meets — it’s meets closer to home, especially outside the GTA.
  • Pool and lane access is the area you’d most like to see improve, and it’s where we’ll keep pushing.

The survey is still open, and every response helps us advocate for you — on pool time, on regional competition, and on keeping the sport affordable. If you haven’t yet, add your voice.

Please share this survey with any adult swimmer you know — we want to hear from everyone, whether they’re attached to a club or not.

New · Get involved

Masters Swimming Ambassador Program

We’ve launched the Swim Ontario Masters Swimming Ambassador Program — a new way for passionate swimmers to help grow the sport in their own club and region. Led by long-time coach and Masters advocate Nancy Black, the Masters’ Swimming Committee would appoint ‘ambassadors’ to each of Swim Ontario’s regions.

Ambassadors outreach to the local Masters swim community, spread the word about meets and clinics, and act as a local point of connection for Masters swimming across the province. They provide regional needs to the Masters Committee as a whole so that all regions of the province are ‘seen’ and heard from.

It’s one of the most direct answers to what the survey asked for: more visibility for Masters, stronger clubs, and a friendlier on-ramp for beginners or those new to Masters swimming.

If that sounds like you — or if you know someone who would be great at this — please contact Nancy asap at mastersambassador@swimontario.com.

Coaching · Blue Mountain · 1–4 October

Coaching this fall: CSCA National Conference & Swim Ontario sessions

The 2026 CSCA National Swim Coaches Conference runs 1–4 October at Blue Mountain Resort. This year, rather than hosting a separate conference, Swim Ontario is bringing its Ontario Swimming Community sessions to Blue Mountain as part of the event.

The CSCA program features a Performance Coaching stream (1–4 Oct) and an Age Group Coaching stream (3–4 Oct), led by internationally respected coaches and sport-science experts.

▪ Swim Ontario sessions at Blue Mountain

  • Saturday, 3 October — Club Leadership workshop & dinner. For club board members, administrators, presidents, and other volunteers. Participation for up to two club representatives is already included as part of each club’s Swim Ontario Community Development Fee. Additional representatives may attend for a fee of $50.
  • Sunday, 4 October — Officials Session & Masters Session. A Swim Ontario Officials Session, plus a dedicated Masters Session.

Registration for the Swim Ontario Saturday and Sunday sessions will be posted and sent to clubs soon.

▪ Sunday’s Masters Session: Bridging the Gap

The Swim Ontario Masters Session tackles Masters-specific coaching head-on with Bridging the Gap: Practical Application & Sustainable Coaching for Masters Athletes — a stream running 10:00 AM–1:00 PM that moves from sports science, to on-deck practice, to shaping the future of Masters coaching in Ontario.

Time Session
10:00–10:50 AM Block 1 · The Physiology of the Adult Athlete
Training the aging body: how to adapt traditional energy-system work — aerobic capacity and lactate-threshold training — for Masters swimmers with varied athletic backgrounds, slower recovery times, and different biomechanical limits.
10:50–11:40 AM Block 2 · The Art of Managing “Fitness vs. Racing” Lanes
How to write a single workout that serves the competitive few while keeping the fitness and wellness majority engaged, valued, and moving — practical lane-management strategies, community building, and creative yardage modifications.
11:40–11:50 AM Short break
11:50 AM–1:00 PM Block 3 · Roundtable & Collaboration: Building the Masters Pathway
An interactive, collaborative workshop on what a permanent, flexible, and cost-effective Masters-specific coaching certification stream could look like — and a chance for grassroots coaches to help shape Swim Ontario’s future coaching direction.

Session times are not final — please refer to the final package.

Blue Mountain has a conference room block ($189–$225/night) held until 3 September — book early.

Member opportunity · Not a Swim Ontario event

Community opportunity: Cancun Masters Swim Camp

Here’s one to warm you up. A group of Ontario coaches is running a Masters Swim Camp in Cancun, 20–27 February 2027 — a week of training in an outdoor 50-metre pool, all-inclusive at the Dreams Vista Cancun resort. Last year’s first camp drew swimmers from three different Masters clubs, and the biggest takeaway wasn’t the coaching or the sunshine — it was connecting with swimmers from other clubs.

This year they’d love to open it up to more clubs across Ontario. Let’s be honest — there are worse places to start your long-course build for Worlds 2027 than a 50-metre pool in Cancun in February.

Cheer her on · English Channel · 18 August

One of our own takes on the English Channel

A member of our Masters community, Becky Yuan, is set to take on one of open water’s greatest tests — the English Channel, Dover to Calais (33 km) — on 18 August. It’s a fitting challenge for an athlete who in 2022 became the first Chinese Canadian to swim across Lake Erie, and followed it with a Lake Ontario crossing in 2023.

This is her third major channel attempt — both her 2024 English Channel and 2025 Catalina Channel swims were turned back by severe weather rather than fitness. Every stroke also raises funds for The Alexa Trust, a UK charity supporting families with babies in neonatal intensive care.

Let’s get behind her: follow along, send some encouragement, and chip in if you can.
